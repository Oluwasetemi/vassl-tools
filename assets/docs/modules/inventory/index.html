Inventory

The Inventory module is the product catalogue for Video Access Security Solutions Ltd. It tracks what products exist, how much stock is on hand, and whether stock is above or below minimum levels.

Open with Cmd+1 (macOS) / Ctrl+1 (Windows).

Product list

Section titled “Product list”

The main panel shows all products as a table with:

ColumnDescription
SKUUnique product code
NameProduct display name
CategoryOptional grouping (e.g. Cameras, Networking)
UnitUnit of measure (pcs, box, roll, etc.)
In StockCurrent total quantity across all stock entries
Min LevelMinimum acceptable stock quantity
StatusGreen = healthy · Amber = low · Red = critical

Stock status colours

Section titled “Stock status colours”

Stock status is calculated from the current quantity relative to the minimum stock level:

ColourStatusCondition
GreenHealthyStock > Min × 1.2
AmberLowMin < Stock ≤ Min × 1.2
RedCriticalStock ≤ Min (and Min > 0)
—No alertMin stock level = 0

The 20 % buffer above minimum (the Amber zone) gives advance warning before stock actually hits the critical threshold.

Note

Products with a Min Stock Level of 0 show no status badge — they are considered untracked for restock purposes.

Filtering

Section titled “Filtering”

Use the search bar at the top of the panel (Cmd+F / Ctrl+F) to filter products by name, SKU, or category in real time. Press the × button to clear the filter.

Adding a product

Section titled “Adding a product”

  1. Press Cmd+N (macOS) / Ctrl+N (Windows) or click + New Product.

  2. Fill in the product form:

    FieldRequiredNotes
    SKUYesUnique across all products. Immutable after creation.
    NameYesDisplay name shown in lists and quotations
    CategoryNoOptional grouping (e.g. Cameras, Networking)
    UnitYesUnit of measure — e.g. pcs, box, roll, metre
    Model #NoManufacturer model number
    Part #NoManufacturer part number
    Duty %NoImport duty percentage applied in price calculations (default 0)
    Initial StockNoStarting quantity on hand (create mode only)
    Min Stock LevelNoThreshold for restock alerts (default 0)
    DescriptionNoLonger product notes or specifications
    Preferred SupplierNoSelect from your supplier list

  3. Press Enter or click Save.

Note

SKU is immutable after creation. Choose a consistent format — for example HIK-DS2CD2143G2 for Hikvision model DS2CD2143G2, or CAM-IP-2MP for a generic 2 MP IP camera.

Editing a product

Section titled “Editing a product”

Right-click any product row and select Edit Product. In edit mode:

  • SKU is shown as a read-only label (cannot be changed after creation)
  • Current Stock is editable — changing this value creates an automatic Adjustment stock entry for the delta
  • All other fields behave the same as in create mode

Adding stock (New Entry)

Section titled “Adding stock (New Entry)”

Stock is added through stock entries — each entry records a delivery or acquisition event.

  1. Select a product row in the list.

  2. Click + New Entry (enabled when a product is selected).

  3. Fill in the stock entry form:

    FieldRequiredNotes
    QuantityYesUnits received (can be negative for Adjustments)
    Unit Cost (USD)YesCost per unit at time of receipt
    SupplierNoWho supplied this batch
    Acquisition TypeYesRestock · Project · Adjustment
    Acquired AtYesDate received
    Invoice RefNoInvoice or purchase order reference
    NotesNoAny additional notes

  4. Save. The product’s In Stock count updates immediately.

Acquisition types explained:

TypeWhen to use
RestockStandard purchase from a supplier
ProjectStock received for a specific project
AdjustmentManual correction — use a negative quantity to remove stock

Restock Alerts tab

Section titled “Restock Alerts tab”

Switch to the Restock Alerts tab (next to Products) to see only the products that are in Critical or Low status. This is a filtered view — no separate data.

Context menu

Section titled “Context menu”

Right-click any product row for quick actions:

  • Edit Product — open the edit form
  • Price History — jump to that product’s price history in the Price Book
  • Add Price Entry — open the Price Book entry form for that product
