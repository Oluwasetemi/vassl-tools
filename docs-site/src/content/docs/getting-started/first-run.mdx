## Creating the administrator account

When VASSL is launched for the very first time, the database is empty and there are no user accounts. A setup screen will appear asking you to create the **administrator account**. This is a one-time step.

<Steps>
1. Enter a **username** for the administrator account.
2. Enter and confirm a **password**.
3. Click **Create Account**.
4. VASSL will open the login screen.
</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
Choose a strong password for the administrator account — it has full access to every module and can manage all other user accounts.
</Aside>

## Logging in

Every time VASSL opens, you will be presented with the login screen.

<Steps>
1. Enter your **username**.
2. Enter your **password**.
3. Press **Enter** or click **Log In**.
</Steps>

If your credentials are correct and your account is active, VASSL will open to the Inventory module.

<Aside type="note">
If you enter the wrong password, the login form will show an error. There is no account lockout — you can try again immediately.
</Aside>

## Logging out

To switch users or leave the machine unattended, log out from the session:

- Press **Cmd+Shift+L** (macOS) / **Ctrl+Shift+L** (Windows), or
- Open **Settings** and click **Log Out** at the bottom of the Security section.

Logging out returns you to the login screen. The application stays open.

## Recommended first steps

Once you are logged in as administrator, do these things before anyone else starts using VASSL:

<Steps>
1. **Set the USD → JMD exchange rate** — open **Settings** (`Cmd/Ctrl+,`) and enter the current rate in the **Price Book** section. This rate is used as the default for new quotations and for displaying JMD equivalents.

2. **Create accounts for your team** — in **Settings → Admin**, add a user account for each person who will use VASSL. Assign each person only the modules and permissions they need.

3. **Add your suppliers** — go to the Suppliers module (`Cmd/Ctrl+4`) and add the suppliers you procure from. This allows you to assign preferred suppliers to products.

4. **Add your products** — go to the Inventory module (`Cmd/Ctrl+1`) and add your product catalogue. Set SKU, category, unit, and minimum stock level for each.

5. **Set up your price book** — go to the Price Book module (`Cmd/Ctrl+3`) and add cost prices and markups for each product.

6. **Create your first quotation** — go to Quotations (`Cmd/Ctrl+2`), create a project, and build a quotation from your price book.
</Steps>
