The Price Book module stores the selling price for every product. Each entry records the **cost price**, **duty percentage**, **markup percentage**, and the resulting **selling price**. Full history is kept — every pricing change is recorded with a date.

Open with **Cmd+3** (macOS) / **Ctrl+3** (Windows).

## Price table

The main panel shows the **current** (most recent) price for each product:

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Column</th><th>Description</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Product</td><td>Product name</td></tr>
    <tr><td>SKU</td><td>Product code</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Cost Price</td><td>Your cost in USD or JMD</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Duty Cost</td><td>Import duty in USD (derived from Cost × Duty %)</td></tr>

  </tbody>
</table>

### Selling price formula

```
Selling Price = Cost × (1 + Duty% / 100) × (1 + Markup% / 100)
```

**Duty %** is a property of the product itself (set in the Inventory module when adding or editing a product). It is not entered per price entry — the form reads it automatically.

**Example:** Cost = $100 USD, Duty = 40 %, Markup = 30 %
```
Landed cost = 100 × 1.40 = $140
Selling     = 140 × 1.30 = $182
```

### Filtering

Use the search bar at the top to filter by product name or SKU in real time.

## Adding a price entry

Price entries are tied to a specific product. You must select the product first.

<Steps>
1. Click a product row in the Price Book table to select it.
2. Click **+ New Entry** in the toolbar (enabled when a product is selected), or right-click the row and choose **Add Price Entry**.
3. Fill in the form:

   <table>
     <thead><tr><th>Field</th><th>Required</th><th>Notes</th></tr></thead>
     <tbody>
       <tr><td>Quantity</td><td>Yes</td><td>Quantity this price applies to (e.g. <code>1</code>, <code>10</code>)</td></tr>
       <tr><td>Cost Price</td><td>Yes</td><td>Landed cost — toggle between USD and JMD with the currency button</td></tr>
       <tr><td>Duty %</td><td>—</td><td>Read-only; taken from the product record in Inventory</td></tr>
       <tr><td>Markup %</td><td>Yes</td><td>Percentage markup over landed cost (default from settings)</td></tr>
       <tr><td>Selling Price</td><td>—</td><td>Auto-calculated and displayed in green; not editable</td></tr>
     </tbody>
   </table>

4. Press **Enter** or click **Save**.
</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
Always add a **new entry** rather than editing an existing one when prices change. This preserves the full pricing history for auditing and accurate historical quotation reference.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note">
To change the Duty % for a product, edit the product in the **Inventory** module. The updated duty percentage will take effect in all new price entries.
</Aside>

### Currency toggle

The Cost Price field supports two currencies:

- **USD** — US dollars (default)
- **JMD** — Jamaican dollars

The currency selection and the exchange rate are persisted in Settings (`pricebook.currency` and `pricebook.exchange_rate`, default 156.00 JMD/USD). When JMD is entered, it is stored converted to USD.

## Price history

Switch to the **History** tab or right-click a product row and select **Price History** to see all previous price entries for that product in chronological order. This is useful for:

- Tracking cost and selling price trends over time
- Understanding why a historical quotation used a particular price
- Comparing landed costs across different purchase periods

## Relationship with Inventory and Quotations

- The product's **Duty %** lives in Inventory and flows into every new price entry automatically
- **Selling prices** from the most recent price entry are used to pre-fill line items when building quotations
- Stock entries created from quotation approvals are recorded back in Inventory against the originating project
